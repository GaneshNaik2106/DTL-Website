  • What are Optical Fibres?
  • Optical fibers are thin, transparent strands made of glass or plastic, guiding light through total internal reflection. With a core to carry light and a cladding layer to maintain its path, they minimize signal loss. Crucial for high-speed data transmission in telecommunications and internet networks, they also serve in medical imaging, sensors, and industrial applications. Their efficiency and versatility make them vital components in modern communication and technology infrastructure.
