Level 3: Types of Optical FIbres

Welcome to Level 3! In this level, you'll explore the Types of optical fibres.

Topics covered:

  • What are the different types of Optical Fibres?

  • There are several types of optical fibers designed for various applications:

    Single-mode fibers:
    These fibers have a small core diameter, allowing only one mode of light to propagate. They are ideal for long-distance telecommunications and high-speed data transmission.

    Multimode fibers:
    With a larger core diameter, multimode fibers can carry multiple modes of light simultaneously. They are commonly used in shorter-distance communications and LANs.

    Plastic optical fibers (POFs):
    Made of plastic materials, POFs are more flexible and cost-effective than glass fibers. They are often used in automotive, home networking, and lighting applications.

    Graded-index fibers:
    These fibers have a core with a refractive index that decreases gradually from the center outward. They minimize modal dispersion, making them suitable for high-speed data transmission.

    Photonic crystal fibers:
    These specialty fibers feature a patterned microstructure that allows for unique light-guiding properties, enabling applications in sensing, nonlinear optics, and telecommunications.
